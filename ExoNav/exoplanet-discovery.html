Exoplanet Discovery Methods

Here are the detailed explanations of different methods used for exoplanet discovery:

1. Transit Photometry

This method detects distant planets by measuring the dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it. It's one of the most successful and widely used techniques.

2. Radial Velocity

This technique measures the tiny wobbles in a star's motion caused by the gravitational tug of an orbiting planet. It helps in determining the planet's mass and orbit.

3. Direct Imaging

Direct imaging involves capturing actual images of exoplanets by blocking out the star's light. It's the most challenging method but allows for observation of the planet's atmosphere.
